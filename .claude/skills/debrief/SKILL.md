# Meeting Debrief

When the user wants to debrief after a meeting, process a transcript, or extract action items from a conversation.

## Trigger phrases
- /debrief
- "debrief that call"
- "what came out of that meeting"
- "process the transcript"
- "pull the your meeting transcript tool notes from [meeting]"

## What to do

1. Find the meeting. Check your meeting transcript tool for the most recent meeting, or search by name/org if specified. Pull the full transcript.

2. Extract and organize:

**Key decisions made**
- [Decision]: [Who decided, any conditions]

**Commitments (who owes what)**
- [Person] will [action] by [date/timeframe]
- Flag any commitments made by the user specifically

**Intelligence gathered**
- [Anything learned about the org, person, budget, timeline, competition, internal dynamics]
- Pain points expressed (use their words)
- Buying signals or objections

**Relationship notes**
- Rapport level (cold/warming/warm/strong)
- Personal details mentioned (remember for next interaction)
- Communication preferences observed

**Next steps**
- Immediate (today/tomorrow): [specific actions]
- This week: [follow-up items]
- Scheduled: [anything with a date commitment]

**Content opportunities**
- Anything from this conversation that could become a LinkedIn post, case study, or proof point (one sentence each)

3. Draft the follow-up. Write a follow-up email or message appropriate to the conversation. Keep it tight, reference something specific from the call, and include any committed next steps.

4. Produce a knowledge graph update block:
```
GRAPH UPDATE:
- Node: [person/org] — update [fields]
- Edge: [relationship changes]
- Follow-up: [action] by [date]
```

## Voice
Analytical, not narrative. Extract signal from noise. Prioritize what's actionable over what's interesting.

## Do NOT
- Summarize the entire conversation chronologically
- Include small talk or pleasantries from the transcript
- Speculate about things not discussed
- Reveal internal methodology or frameworks in the follow-up draft
