# Discovery Prep

Pre-meeting intelligence gathering for upcoming calls with prospects, partners, or clients.

## Trigger phrases
- /discover [person or org]
- "prep me for [meeting/call]"
- "what do I know about [person/org]"
- "research [org] before our call"

## What to do

1. Check internal sources first:
   - Knowledge graph: existing nodes, relationship history, pipeline status, pain points
   - Granola: past meeting transcripts with this person/org
   - Past conversations: any prior context or decisions
   - LinkedIn CSV: connection status, mutual connections, their role/company

2. Check external sources:
   - Web search: recent news, leadership changes, funding, press releases
   - Their website: what they do, who they serve, recent announcements
   - LinkedIn (if URL available from CSV): current role, career history

3. Present the brief:

**[Person] at [Org]**

**Relationship status:** [New / Building / Warm / Strong] Last contact: [date]

**What they do:** [2 sentences max]

**Recent news:** [Anything in last 90 days]

**What I already know:** [Summary from past meetings, notes, graph]

**Their likely pain points:**
- [Based on their sector, size, and any prior conversations]

**Conversation approach:**
- Open with: [Something specific to reference, shows you did homework]
- Ask about: [2-3 discovery questions tailored to their situation]
- Listen for: [Buying signals or objections to expect]

**Relevant proof points:**
- [Which AIFC proof points match their sector/size]

**Watch out for:**
- [Any risks, sensitive topics, or things to avoid based on prior context]

## Voice
Brief, practical, tactical. This is a cheat sheet, not a research paper.

## Do NOT
- Include generic company boilerplate from their website
- List every proof point (pick the 2-3 most relevant)
- Reveal the methodology or framework names in suggested conversation points
